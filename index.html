Helping Workers in Michigan

Many are in financial distress due to Covid-19 business closures.

Service Workers Need Our Help

With so many businesses in our community shutting down in response to Covid-19, most service workers have suddenly lost a reliable income. To support them, this site can be used to raise funds for local workers at restaurants, bars, cafes, gyms, salons, spas, markets, shops, studios, theaters, jobsites and other small businesses impacted by these closures.

Our initial coverage includes Oakland County with a goal of expanding into other counties and regions across Michigan.

If you are able to support a local business in Oakland County, you can find them below and contribute to them directly.

Tell me more...

In order to slow the spread of the coronavirus, our state and local government is advising restaurants, bars, cafes, nightclubs, movie theaters, small theater houses, and concert venues to close. Elsewhere across our economy, closures are even more comprehensive.

While this is prudent from a public health perspective, these closures will have a real and immediate economic impact to service workers across industries. It could mean missed rent or inability to pay for basic needs for many.

While interest free loans are a good place to start, we believe we can do more to help those affected, and our goal is to raise funds for businesses in our community affected by this public health crisis.

We cannot all be working alongside all of the heroic healthcare workers testing and treating coronavirus patients. Our goal with this project is to enable every member of our community to do something today to mitigate the economic impact suffered by a huge number of the service workers that help fuel our economy everyday.
